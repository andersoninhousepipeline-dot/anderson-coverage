Twist-only vs MedGenome-only gap-size profile

Twist-only gaps are overwhelmingly narrow — median 1 bp, mean 10.6 bp, max 6,186 bp across 87,098 micro-intervals; mostly probe-boundary rounding, not missing genes. MedGenome-only regions are far fewer (1,328) but larger on average — median 78 bp, mean 142 bp, max 2,964 bp — and sit entirely outside Twist's target design.

Definitions & Method

Merged intervalsPer-file bedtools merge on sorted intervals; both files had zero internal overlaps, so merged counts equal raw line counts.
Overlap (shared)bedtools intersect between the two merged files — bases physically targeted by both designs.
Twist-onlybedtools subtract -a twist -b medgenome — bases Twist targets that MedGenome does not.
MedGenome-onlybedtools subtract -a medgenome -b twist — bases MedGenome targets that Twist does not.
UnionTwist bases + MedGenome bases − overlap bases; the non-redundant combined footprint.
Gene attributionMedGenome's BED carries no gene-symbol column, so gap regions are annotated by intersecting Twist-only/MedGenome-only intervals back against Twist's own 4th-column annotation (first non-transcript token per interval).
